# Chain of title: 3594 E Palmer St, Gilbert AZ (individual-to-LLC conveyance)

The subject parcel is Lot 38, Seville Parcel 3, a Shea Homes subdivision in Gilbert. Every instrument on this parcel in the corpus is demo-only synthesized — disclosed per-document in the Proof Drawer — so the chain below illustrates examiner workflow rather than reproducing a real recorded history.

By warranty deed recorded September 14, 2017, the Rivera Family Trust, dated June 1, 2005, conveyed the property to Maria Elena Delgado, an unmarried woman [20170200001]. Concurrently, Delgado executed a purchase-money deed of trust in favor of Quicken Loans Inc, with MERS as beneficiary as nominee [20170200002]; $284,000 original principal; MIN 1000256-0003456789-0. The note was later sold to Rocket Mortgage LLC (Quicken's successor-in-interest), but no assignment of deed of trust has been recorded in this parcel's corpus.

On November 8, 2022, a three-document same-day transaction transferred the property to a wholly-owned LLC. First, a Statement of Authority under A.R.S. § 33-432 was recorded [20220200002] designating Maria Elena Delgado as sole member and manager of Delgado Property Holdings LLC (an Arizona limited liability company, ACC entity number AZ-L22XXXXX) and authorizing her to convey real property for the entity. Second, a warranty deed conveyed the property from Maria Elena Delgado individually to Delgado Property Holdings LLC for "$10 and other good and valuable consideration" [20220200003]. Third, an Affidavit of Property Value under A.R.S. § 11-1133 was recorded documenting a full cash value of $485,000 against nominal consideration, exemption code B3 (individual-to-wholly-owned-entity) [20220200004].

The deed of trust was not released, assumed, or assigned at the 2022 conveyance. The loan remains in Maria Elena Delgado's individual name. Due-on-sale clauses under 12 USC § 1701j-3 are technically available to the lender on any conveyance to a non-natural person, including a grantor-owned LLC; the Garn-St Germain exemptions for spouses, children, and inter vivos trusts do not extend to LLCs. In practice, servicers rarely enforce due-on-sale on owner-occupied single-family pass-through LLCs, but the exposure exists and no lender acknowledgment has been recorded.
