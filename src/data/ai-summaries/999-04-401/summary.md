# Chain of title: 3722 E Parkview Ave, Gilbert AZ (tax-sale REO scenario)

The subject parcel is Lot 56, Seville Parcel 3. Every instrument on this parcel in the corpus is demo-only synthesized — disclosed per-document in the Proof Drawer — so the chain below illustrates examiner workflow rather than reproducing a real recorded history.

By warranty deed recorded March 21, 2005, Shea Homes Arizona LP conveyed the property to James D and Tammy L Henderson, husband and wife, as joint tenants with right of survivorship [20050200001]. Concurrently, the Hendersons executed a purchase-money deed of trust in favor of Countrywide Home Loans Inc, with MERS as beneficiary as nominee; $287,955 original principal; MIN 1000157-0002876543-2 [20050200002]. Countrywide was acquired by Bank of America in July 2008. No assignment of deed of trust to Bank of America or any securitization trust has been recorded in this parcel's corpus.

The Hendersons fell delinquent on 2018 property taxes. The Maricopa County Treasurer sold the delinquent tax lien at auction on February 12, 2019, to Tucson Investments LLC for $3,847 plus penalties and interest (Certificate of Purchase 2019-XXXXXX). The Hendersons did not redeem within the three-year redemption period under A.R.S. § 42-18152. Tucson Investments filed a quiet-title action and obtained judgment on May 20, 2022 (Maricopa County Superior Court case CV2022-XXXXXX). By Treasurer's Deed recorded June 15, 2022, the Maricopa County Treasurer conveyed fee title to Tucson Investments LLC under A.R.S. § 42-18267 [20220200005].

A Treasurer's Deed issued under A.R.S. § 42-18267 conveys an estate in fee simple free and clear of all claims, liens, and encumbrances EXCEPT (a) other tax liens, (b) restrictions, easements, and covenants running with the land, and (c) claims of the United States subject to federal law. The 2005 Countrywide deed of trust [20050200002] was therefore extinguished by operation of law on June 15, 2022. No release was ever recorded, and no successor-in-interest quit-claim has been obtained from Bank of America or any Countrywide securitization trust. The deed of trust appears open in a raw index query but is substantively cleared.

On January 30, 2023, Tucson Investments flipped the property to James T Bryant by special warranty deed for $425,000 [20230200002]. The special warranty limits the grantor's warranty to claims arising by, through, or under Tucson Investments and expressly does not warrant against claims predating Tucson's acquisition via the Treasurer's Deed. Concurrently, Bryant executed a new purchase-money deed of trust in favor of AmeriSave Mortgage Corporation, with MERS as beneficiary as nominee; $340,000 original principal; MIN 1000267-0004567890-1 [20230200003].
